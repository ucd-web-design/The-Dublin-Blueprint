The 3-Day Blueprint

Planning a trip to Dublin for a weekend? We’ve got you covered. This 3-day itinerary is designed to help you experience the very best of the city without feeling rushed or overwhelmed. Whether you're here for culture, food, or a bit of craic, this guide brings everything together in a simple, enjoyable way.


And if you're lucky… you might even get a sunny weekend (we can dream, right?)

Day 1 — Dublin City Highlights

Morning

Begin your morning at Beanhive Coffee on Dawson Street, a local favourite, where you can enjoy a traditional full Irish breakfast. You’ll need it for the day ahead. From there, it’s just a short walk to Trinity College where you can explore the famous Book of Kells, one of Ireland’s greatest cultural treasures. Just around the corner, stop by the iconic Molly Malone Statue for a quick photo. Continue on to Dublin Castle, where you can soak up centuries of Irish history and admire the beautiful architecture.

Afternoon

Afterwards, wander down Grafton Street, Dublin’s main shopping street, known for its lively atmosphere and street musicians. While you're there, grab a famous spice bag from Xian Street Food, which is basically a Dublin essential at this point. Take your food over to St. Stephen’s Green, but just keep an eye out for the cheeky seagulls who might try to rob your lunch! Next, visit the National Museum of Ireland, where you can explore over 4,000 years of Irish history - and it’s completely free.

Evening

As evening approaches, head to Sophie’s Rooftop Restaurant for dinner and enjoy stunning views over the city. Finish your night in Temple Bar. It’s touristy, yeah, but worth seeing. For live traditional music, stop into Merchant’s Arch, where the atmosphere is always buzzing.

Day 2 — Coastal Escape to Howth

Morning

Take a break from the city and enjoy a scenic day trip to the seaside village of Howth. Hop on the DART from Dublin and enjoy the coastal views on your way. Once you arrive, start your morning at Howth Market, where you can pick up breakfast, coffee, and maybe even a few souvenirs. Before setting off, grab some snacks and put together a picnic - you’ll thank yourself later.

Afternoon

Next, take on the Howth Cliff Walk Loop (around 2 hours), one of the most beautiful walks in Ireland. Along the way, you’ll see breathtaking views of the sea and the iconic lighthouse. After your walk, head down to the Tiny Hidden Beach at the bottom of the cliffs. Fair warning, the way down (and especially back up) is not for the faint-hearted, but it’s absolutely worth it. It’s super quiet, sheltered from the wind, and a perfect hidden spot to relax and watch the sunset.

Evening

End your day at Howth Harbour with some fresh seafood for dinner before heading back to Dublin. And of course… no day in Ireland is complete without a pint when you get back!

Day 3 — Culture & History

Morning

For your last day, take it a bit slower. Start your morning at Bread 41, one of Dublin’s best bakeries, and treat yourself to a coffee and a freshly baked pastry.

Afernoon

Then, experience two of Ireland’s most famous exports with a Jameson Distillery and Guinness Storehouse tour — a fun and interactive way to learn about Irish whiskey and stout without feeling like a history lesson. For lunch, keep it casual with a stop at Bambino’s, known for its delicious pizza slices. In the afternoon, visit Kilmainham Gaol, a former prison that played a key role in Irish history. It offers powerful insights into Ireland’s past and its struggle for independence.

Evening

For your final evening, book a dinner at The Church, a restaurant set inside an old church, which is pretty cool on its own. Enjoy traditional Irish food along with live music and dancing every evening from 6:30 pm. Just be sure to book in advance, as it’s very popular!

Final Thoughts

Three days in Dublin is the perfect amount of time to experience the city’s highlights, enjoy amazing food, and take in some unforgettable views. If you’re staying longer, we highly recommend taking a full-day trip to the Cliffs of Moher, one of Ireland’s most breathtaking natural attractions (you can find more details in our Top Attractions section). No matter how long you stay, Dublin has a way of making you feel right at home - and hopefully, this guide helps you enjoy every minute of it. ☘️
