Dublin's Essential Guide 2026

The best Restaurants, Bars, and Cafes in the Fair City

Fine Dining

Michelin Star

Chapter One

Located on Parnell Square, this is the pinnacle of Irish fine dining. Known for its impeccable service and the famous laminated treacle brioche.

Modern European

Dax Restaurant

Consistently rated as one of the best for fine dining in Dublin, offering a sophisticated French-influenced menu in a Georgian basement.

Casual Eats & Trendy Spots

Quick Bite

Howth Market

Howth Market is a fantastic day out and offers a unique experience with a vast array of organic produce, freshly baked goods, jewelry, clothing, antiques and much much more...

Bambino's

A NYC style slice shop selling Premium Slices & Squares, and Full Pies. A must stop for any Dublin trip!

Asian Inspired

Xian Street Food

A cult favorite with multiple locations around Dublin's city centre. Don't miss the classic Spice Bag and hand-pulled noodles!

Italian

Grano

Located in Stoneybatter, this cozy spot serves authentic, handmade Calabrian pasta. Booking weeks in advance is essential!

Seafood

The Fish Shop

A refined take on fish and chips in Smithfield with a daily-changing menu based on the morning's catch.

International and Irish Blend

Sophie's

From wood-fired pizzas and classic salads to succulent steaks and fish specials, Sophie's has something for everyone!

The Church

Here you can enjoy coffee, drinks, great Irish food and traditional Irish music in one of Ireland’s most unique venues based in the heart of Dublin City.

Top Bars & Pubs

Speakeasy

Vintage Cocktail Club (VCC)

A hidden gem in Temple Bar. Ring the bell at the unmarked black door for a 1920s-style cocktail experience.

Traditional

The Long Hall

One of the most beautiful Victorian pubs in the city. Go for the history, stay for what is arguably the best Guinness in Dublin.

Merchant's Arch

Whether you’re stopping in for a meal, drinks with friends, or planning a group booking, the space is designed to suit every occasion.

Craft & Vibes

Fidelity

An audiophile bar in Smithfield with a custom sound system and a massive selection of craft beer and cocktails on tap.

Best Cafes

Breakfast and Brunch

Bean Hive

Specialty coffee, yummy treats, and delicious lunch, what more can you ask for!

Brother Hubbard

Middle Eastern-inspired brunch that has become a Dublin institution. Their Capel Street location is vibrant and spacious.

Bakery

Scéal Bakery

Newly returned to South Circular Road, serving world-class sourdough and seasonal pastries that often sell out by noon.

Bread 41

A high-end bakery and cafe with a terffic mix of sweet and savory options!
