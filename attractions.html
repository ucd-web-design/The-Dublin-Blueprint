Top Attractions

The must-sees, plus a few that make Dublin feel special.

Attractions Activities Hidden Gems

Discover some of Dublin’s most iconic landmarks and experiences — from historic cathedrals and cultural museums to lively streets and scenic walks. Whether you’re interested in history, architecture, or just exploring the city’s atmosphere, these are the places that truly capture what Dublin is all about.

Dublinia

An interactive museum that brings Dublin’s Viking and medieval history to life with immersive exhibits. Great for families and history lovers.

Location: Dublinia, St Michaels Hill Christ Church, 5 Meath St, Saint Catherine's, Dublin 8, D08 VE80.

Price: Paid entrance = €18 adult / €11.50 child

Visit website.

St Patrick’s Cathedral

Ireland’s largest cathedral, dating back to 1220, known for its stunning architecture and connection to Jonathan Swift.

Location: St Patrick's Cathedral, St Patrick's Close, Dublin, D08 H6X3.

Price: €11.50 adult / €5.50 child

Visit website.

Trinity College & Book of Kells

Ireland’s most famous university, home to the 9th-century Book of Kells and the breathtaking Long Room Library.

Location: Trinity College Dublin

Price: €25.50 adult / €13.50 child

Visit website.

The Spire

A striking 120-metre stainless steel landmark located on O’Connell Street. Built in 2003, it symbolises modern Dublin and is one of the city’s most recognisable meeting points.

Location: O’Connell Street

Price: Free

The GPO

Historic site of the 1916 Easter Rising, where you can still see bullet marks from the rebellion on its exterior walls. Inside, the immersive “Witness History” exhibition brings this pivotal moment in Irish history to life.

Location: General Post Office, O'Connell Street Lower, North City, Dublin 1, D01 F5P2.

Price: Free to enter, Museum= €15 adult / €7.50 child

Visit website.

Guinness Storehouse

Explore Ireland’s most famous beer and enjoy views from the Gravity Bar.

Location: Guinness Storehouse, St. James's Gate, Dublin 8, D08 VF8H.

Price: €20–€30

Visit website.

Temple Bar

Dublin’s most famous cultural quarter with pubs and live music.

Location: Dublin 2

Price: Free

National Museum of Ireland

Discover Irish history, artefacts, and ancient treasures.

Location: National Museum of Ireland, Merrion Street, Dublin 2.

Price: Free

Visit website.

Top Activities

Looking to experience more than just the sights? These activities offer unforgettable ways to explore Dublin and beyond.

Viking Splash Tour

See Dublin from land and water on this unique Viking-themed tour. Travel through the city in an amphibious vehicle before splashing into the Grand Canal, all while guides share fun and engaging stories.

Location: Viking Splash Tours, St Stephen's Green, North, Dublin 2. (Located opposite the Cliff Townhouse and just behind the taxi rank and water fountain).

Price: €35 adult / €29 child

Visit website.

Bray & Howth Cliff Walks

Escape the city with scenic coastal walks offering stunning sea views, fresh air, and well-marked trails.

Location: Bray Head Hike & Howth Cliff Walk

Price: Free

Phoenix Park (Deer Spotting)

Explore Phoenix Park, the largest enclosed city park in Europe, and spot wild deer roaming freely. It’s a peaceful escape from the city and perfect for walking, cycling, or a picnic.

Location: Phoenix Park, Parkgate Street, Dublin 7.(Deers are usually spotted by the Papal Cross)

Price: Free

Riverdance

Experience Ireland’s most famous dance show, combining traditional Irish music and high-energy choreography. Riverdance is a must-see cultural performance that captures the spirit of Ireland.

Location: Gaiety Theatre, South King Street, Dublin 2.

Price: From €26.50

Visit website.

Cliffs of Moher Day Trip

Take a day trip from Dublin to one of Ireland’s most breathtaking natural wonders. The Cliffs of Moher rise dramatically above the Atlantic Ocean, offering incredible views and unforgettable photo opportunities. Fans of Harry Potter may also recognise the cliffs from scenes filmed here, adding a magical touch to the experience.

Location: Cliffs of Moher, Liscannor, Co. Clare, Ireland V95 KN9T.

Price: Approx. €60–€80 depending on Tour Agencies. (Multiple options on websites such as GetYourGuide and Viator, as well as private ones such as Paddywagon Tours).

Hidden Gems

Looking to explore beyond the usual tourist spots? These hidden gems offer a more local and unique side of Dublin.

Tiny Hidden Beach

Tucked away along the Howth Cliff Walk, this small hidden beach is a peaceful escape from the crowds. If you’re lucky, you might even spot a seal swimming nearby or relaxing along the shore.

Location: Howth Cliff Walk

Price: Free

Stoneybatter

One of Dublin’s trendiest neighbourhoods, Stoneybatter is known for its independent cafés, local pubs, and creative atmosphere. It’s the perfect place to wander, grab a coffee, and experience a more local side of the city.

Location: Stoneybatter, Dublin 7

Price: Free

Love Lane

A colourful hidden alley in Temple Bar, Love Lane is filled with vibrant street art, tiles, and quotes about love. It’s a small but charming spot that’s perfect for photos and a quick wander.

Location: Love Lane, Crampton Court, Temple Bar, Dublin 2.

Price: Free
