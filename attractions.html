Top Attractions

The must-sees, plus a few that make Dublin feel special.

Discover some of Dublin’s most iconic landmarks and experiences — from historic cathedrals and cultural museums to lively streets and scenic walks. Whether you’re interested in history, architecture, or just exploring the city’s atmosphere, these are the places that truly capture what Dublin is all about.

Dublinia

An interactive museum that brings Dublin’s Viking and medieval history to life with immersive exhibits.
Location: St Michaels Hill, Dublin 8
Price: €18 adult / €11.50 child

St Patrick’s Cathedral

Ireland’s largest cathedral dating back to 1220, known for its stunning architecture and connection to Jonathan Swift.
Location: Dublin 8
Price: €11.50 adult / €5.50 child

Trinity College & Book of Kells

Ireland’s most famous university, home to the 9th-century Book of Kells and the breathtaking Long Room Library.
Price: Free campus / Paid exhibition

The Spire

A striking 120-metre stainless steel landmark on O’Connell Street, symbolising modern Dublin.
Price: Free

General Post Office (GPO)

A historic building central to the 1916 Easter Rising, with an immersive “Witness History” exhibition.
Price: Free entry / Paid exhibition

Guinness Storehouse

Explore the story behind Ireland’s most iconic drink and enjoy panoramic views from the Gravity Bar.
Price: €20–€30

Temple Bar

Dublin’s most famous cultural quarter, full of pubs, live music, and energy.
Price: Free

National Museum of Ireland

Discover Ireland’s history through Viking artefacts, Celtic gold, and ancient exhibits.
Price: Free

Top Activities in Dublin

Viking Splash Tour

See Dublin from land and water in this unique amphibious experience.

Bray & Howth Cliff Walks

Enjoy stunning coastal views and fresh sea air on scenic walks.

Phoenix Park

Spot wild deer and enjoy one of Europe’s largest city parks.

Temple Bar

Known for its lively atmosphere, colourful streets and traditional pubs. A must for soaking up the city’s energy.

Hidden Gems

Tiny Hidden Beach

A peaceful hidden spot along the Howth Cliff Walk.

Stoneybatter

A trendy neighbourhood with cafés and local charm.

Love Lane

A colourful hidden alley full of art and quotes.
