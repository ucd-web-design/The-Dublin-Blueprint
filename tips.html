Travel Tips

Essential advice to help you enjoy Dublin safely, cheaply, and stress‑free.

  • Use a Leap Card: Saves money on buses, DART, and Luas.
  • Walking is faster: City centre is compact.
  • Use Free Now for taxis: More reliable than hailing.
  • Airport tip: Dublin Express is cheaper than taxis.
  • Avoid driving in the city centre: Traffic + expensive parking.
  • Rent a car only for day trips: Wicklow, Howth, Glendalough.
  • Left‑side driving: Roundabouts go clockwise.
  • Insurance matters: Always get full coverage.
  • Summer (June–August): Warmest months, long daylight, busiest tourist season.
  • Spring (April–May): Mild weather, fewer crowds — great for sightseeing.
  • Autumn (September–October): Cooler but colourful, ideal for hikes and coastal walks.
  • Winter (November–February): Cold, wet, short days — but Christmas in Dublin is beautiful.
  • Rain is always possible: Pack a light waterproof jacket year‑round.
  • Best overall months: May, June, September — good weather without peak crowds.
  • Currency: Ireland uses the Euro (€).
  • Card is accepted everywhere: Contactless is the norm — cash is rarely needed.
  • ATMs: Avoid Euronet machines; use AIB or Bank of Ireland for lower fees.
  • Food prices: Expect €10–€15 for lunch, €15–€25 for dinner.
  • Pints: €5–€7 in normal pubs, €8–€10 in Temple Bar.
  • Transport: Leap Card gives the best value on buses, DART, and Luas.
  • Budget tip: Free museums and parks help keep costs down.
  • Mind your phone: Pickpockets target nightlife areas.
  • Weather changes fast: Always carry a light jacket.
  • Transport ends early: Plan your journey home.
  • Use Dublin Bus app: More accurate than Google Maps.
  • Best sunset spots: Killiney Hill, Howth Summit.
  • Cheap eats: Boojum, Tolteca, bakeries.
  • Visit early: Beat queues at attractions.

Do

  • Carry a Leap Card.
  • Explore by foot.
  • Try local pubs outside Temple Bar.
  • Visit parks and coastal walks.

Don’t

  • Rely on Google Maps for buses.
  • Leave valuables visible.
  • Expect cheap pints in tourist areas.
  • Underestimate Irish weather.
  • Light rain jacket.
  • Comfortable walking shoes.
  • Portable phone charger.
  • Reusable water bottle.
  • Layers — weather changes fast.
  • Howth: Cliff walk + seafood.
  • Wicklow Mountains: Glendalough + lakes.
  • Malahide: Castle + gardens.
  • Dun Laoghaire: Pier walk + Teddy’s ice cream.
  • Cliffs of Moher: One of Ireland's most breathtaking natural wonders.
