Getting Around Dublin

Getting around Dublin is simple, with plenty of public transport options that make exploring the city easy and affordable. Unless you’re planning day trips outside the city, there’s really no need to rent a car.

Public Transport

Dublin’s main transport options include the Luas (tram), Dublin Bus, and the DART (coastal train). Together, they cover the city centre and surrounding areas.

The Transport for Ireland (TFI) app is especially useful for real-time timetables and planning your journey.

Bike Rental

Renting a bike is a great way to explore the city. Services like Dublin Bikes (Bleeper Bikes) are widely available, and cycling through Phoenix Park is especially popular.

  • Available from newsagents and convenience stores
  • Can be topped up via the Leap Card app or shops like Centra
  • Offers cheaper fares than paying cash
Helpful Tips
  • Press the stop button early on buses
  • Tap on and off the Luas and DART
  • Public transport is much cheaper than taxis

Taxis & Ride Apps

Apps like Free Now and Bolt are widely used, while Uber offers fixed pricing. Taxis can be expensive, so public transport is usually the better option.

Where to Stay in Dublin

Choosing the right location can make your trip much easier. Staying in or near the city centre gives you the best access to attractions, restaurants, and transport links.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Travelodge Plus Dublin City Centre
  • The Mayson Dublin
  • Hilton Garden Inn Dublin City Centre

Luxury & Stylish Stays

  • Zanzibar Locke
  • The Westbury
  • The College Green Hotel

Best Areas to Stay

  • City Centre: Walking distance to major attractions
  • South Side: Safer, lively, full of restaurants and shops
  • Docklands: Modern area with great transport links

Safety in Dublin

Dublin is generally a safe city, especially in central areas and on the south side. However, like any major city, it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings.

  • Safer areas: South side, city centre, Docklands
  • Be cautious: Late at night around certain north inner-city areas
  • Avoid quiet streets late at night if travelling alone
  • Keep valuables secure in busy tourist areas
